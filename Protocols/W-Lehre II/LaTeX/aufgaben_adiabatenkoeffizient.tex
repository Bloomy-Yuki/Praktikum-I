\subsection{Errors and assumptions}
We summarize our errors using the given uncertainties: we add the squares  of the calculated standard deviations and the squared sum of the stat. errors given by the manufacturer and the resolution of the measuring tool. In the case of the scale we use the $0,2$ g given to us by the manufacturer as the first uniform statistical error, while an extra $0.1$ g for the resolution of the scale, which is also assumed to be uniform.
\vspace{1em}

As for the uncertainties of the pressure sensor, we use the $1$ hPa resolution uncertainty as uniformly distributed error and the standard deviation of the measured pressures.
\vspace{1em}

For the micrometer caliper and the vernier caliper, we use the $0,01$ mm, $0,05$mm resolution uncertainties and the standard deviation of the measured diameters while noting the noticeable difference of the measurements (around $2,8 \%$) as systemic error in our measurement method.
\vspace{1em}

Finally, for the Cassy measurements, we use the standard deviation of the measured periods and the $0,01$ s resolution of the Cassy as uniformly distributed error.


$$
\sigma _{m}  =  \frac{0,02}{\sqrt{ 3 }} \text{mm} \\ \oplus \\ 0,057 \text{mm}
$$
$$
\sigma _{p}  =  \frac{1}{\sqrt{ 3 }}\text{hPa} \oplus 0.707\text{hPa}  
$$
$$
\sigma _{D}  =  \left( \frac{0.01}{\sqrt{ 3 }} \oplus  \frac{0.05}{\sqrt{ 3 }}  \right)\text{mm} \oplus 0.251 \text{mm}
$$
$$
\sigma _{T}  =  \frac{0,01}{\sqrt{ 3 }}\text{ s}\oplus 0,0054 \text{ s}
$$

\subsection{Adiabatic coeffcient}

In order to calculate the adiabatic coeffcient, we first neeed to calculate the error propagation of each of the variables using the tables we established eariler. then we Analyse the contribution of each variable so we get an idea as to whether our result is within reasonable bounds. the formula for calculating the adiabatic coeffcient is:


$$
\kappa  =  64 \frac{mV}{p D^{4}T^{2}}
$$

and we can calculate the total error using error propagation of our multiplicative formula, each percentage is multiplied by the exponent to give a clearer view of the contributions:
$$
\left( \frac{\sigma _{\kappa}}{\kappa} \right)^{2}  = \left( \frac{\sigma _{m}}{m} \right)^{2} + \left(  \frac{\sigma _{V}}{V} \right) ^{2} + \left( \frac{\sigma _{p_t}}{p_t} \right)^{2} +\left( \frac{4\sigma _{D}}{D} \right)^{2} + \left( \frac{2\sigma _{T}}{T} \right)^{2}
$$

relative error & $7,6\%$ & $1\%$ & $1\%$ & $0,1\%$ & $6,3\%$  & $4,1\%$ \\ \hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

We can observe from the above table that our largest errors were the diameter of the ball and the period time, which should ultimately contribute to about $8\%$ of the final result.
