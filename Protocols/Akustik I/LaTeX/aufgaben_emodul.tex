We then compute the E-Modul $E$ and the density $\varrho$ for each material using the following formulas:

\begin{equation}
  E = \frac{16}{\pi}  ML \frac{f_{0}^{2}}{D^{2}} \hspace{40pt}  \varrho  =  \frac{M}{\pi L(D/2)^{2}}
\end{equation}

The dominant errors contribution for the E-Modul $E$ is the errors on the frequency $f$ and the diameter $D$, while for the density $\varrho$ the dominant error contribution is the error of the diameter $D$. We also notice a difference between our calculated values and the litrature, which hints at possible systematic error in our measurements, which is possibly due to frequency not being exactly the fundamental frequency, in addition to other factors such as the non uniformity of the materials.
