\begin{aufgabe}{Resonanzfrequenzen mit dem Oszilloskop}
  Tabellieren Sie die gefundenen Resonanzfrequenzen $f_n$. Zur
  Bestimmung der Messunsicherheit auf die $f_n$ führen Sie
  Wiederholungsmessungen durch oder schätzen Sie die Genauigkeit ab,
  mit der Sie die Resonanzfrequenzen mit dem Oszilloskop finden
  können. Bestimmen Sie aus Ihren Messdaten die Schallgeschwindigkeit
  und ihre Unsicherheit. Tragen Sie dazu die $f_n$ gegen $n$ auf und
  führen Sie eine lineare Regression durch.
\end{aufgabe}

We triggered on CH1 which we connected the Function Generator to with the settings Typ= Flanke, Flanke=positiv, Modus=normal, Kopplung=HF Reject. The microphone was connected to CH2. We used the cursor Typ Amplitude to find the maximum of the microphone signal and found the following resonance frequencies:

\begin{table}[H]
\begin{tabular}{@{}c c c c c c@{}}
\hline
 $n$ & 1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 \\
\hline
$f_n$ /Hz & 412 & 818 & 1223 & 1632 & 2039 \\
\hline
$f_n/n$ /Hz & 412 & 409 & 407,6 & 408 & 407,8\\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

We noticed finding the true maximum is not easy to distinguish, which is why we are estimating the true maximum to be within one Hertz. So our statistical error is $\sigma_{stat} = 1Hz$. The systematic error due to the oscilloscope frequency response is estimated at up to 30\% of the measured amplitude near the -3~dB point but since our measurements are well below the bandwidth this error is negligible. \\
To calculate the speed of sound we use the following formula again

\[
v_n = \frac{2 L f_n}{n}.
\]

which gives us the following results:
\begin{verbatim}
  L: 41,975 +/- 0,0357 cm
  f_n / n : 408,00 +/- 0,13 Hz
  v : 342,52 +/- 0,31 m/s
\end{verbatim}

which gives us the mean $\overline{v}=342,52$ m/s and the standard error of the mean $\sigma_{\overline{v},stat}=0,31$ m/s. We performed a linear regression of the measured amplitude versus frequency.

\begin{verbatim}
  R = (406.80+/-0.32) Hz
  b = (4.4+/-1.0) Hz
  chi2/dof = 4.4 / 3
  corr = -0.904529

\end{verbatim}

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.8\textwidth]{reg_f_2.pdf}
    \caption{Linear Regression for the resonances with the oscillator}
    \label{fig:reg_f_2}
\end{figure}

The slope R=406,8 Hz and intercept b=4.4 Hz are given by the fit. The dominant uncertainty comes from estimating the true maximum of the amplitude, which we account for separately as a statistical error.
